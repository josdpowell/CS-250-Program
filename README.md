# CS-250-Program
Reflection Questions:
1. How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
2. How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
3. What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

When approaching software development, I try to avoid the huge risk of failure that comes with a conventional strategy by breaking massive upfront requirements into small, manageable iterations. To interpret user needs, I rely on the Product Backlog as a source of truth and break those high-level needs down into detailed user stories. Creating these stories helps because it forces us to define specific criteria early on. This allows me to write test cases and develop concurrently, ensuring that whatever I am building actually provides value to the customer and keeps the software in a "releasable" state.

Overall, being an effective team member in software development comes down to being cross-functional and self-reliant. So, rather than sticking to what is expected of you, it is important to collaborate with other members and remove barriers. In terms of my personal software development career moving forward, I would probably continue the use of time-boxing and practices like the Daily Scrum to create a consistent workflow. I'd also like to keep using things like the retrospectives, so that I can fail early, learn from mistakes quickly, and adapt to changes as necessary.
